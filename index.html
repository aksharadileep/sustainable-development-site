Sustainable Development Goals

The 17 Global Goals to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all by 2030

1

No Poverty

End poverty in all its forms everywhere by 2030. This goal focuses on social protection systems, equal rights to economic resources, and building resilience of the poor.

10%
Global Target
736M
People in Poverty

Key Targets

  • Eradicate extreme poverty for all people everywhere
  • Reduce at least by half the proportion of people living in poverty
  • Implement social protection systems and measures for all

Poverty is more than lack of income — it's lack of access to basic services, education, and opportunities.

2

Zero Hunger

End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. Ensure access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food year-round.

690M
Hungry People
144M
Stunted Children

Key Targets

  • End hunger and ensure access to safe, nutritious food
  • End all forms of malnutrition
  • Double agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers

Hunger and malnutrition are barriers to sustainable development, creating traps from which people cannot easily escape.

3

Good Health and Well-Being

Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages. This includes reducing maternal mortality, ending preventable deaths, and achieving universal health coverage.

400M
No Basic Healthcare
5.2M
Children deaths/year

Key Targets

  • Reduce global maternal mortality ratio
  • End preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5
  • End epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases

Health is essential for sustainable development and a foundation for thriving communities and economies.

4

Quality Education

Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. Education enables upward socioeconomic mobility and is key to escaping poverty.

258M
Children not in school
617M
Illiterate youth

Key Targets

  • Ensure all children complete free, equitable and quality education
  • Increase number of youth and adults with relevant skills
  • Eliminate gender disparities in education

Education is not only a right but a passport to human development that opens doors to better livelihoods.

5

Gender Equality

Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. Ending all discrimination against women and girls is not only a basic human right, but crucial for sustainable development.

24%
Parliament seats held by women
1 in 3
Women experience violence

Key Targets

  • End all forms of discrimination against all women and girls everywhere
  • Eliminate all forms of violence against women and girls
  • Ensure women's full participation in leadership and decision-making

Gender equality is not only a fundamental human right but a necessary foundation for a peaceful, prosperous and sustainable world.

6

Clean Water and Sanitation

Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Water scarcity affects more than 40% of people globally, an alarming figure projected to rise.

2.2B
Lack safe water
4.2B
Lack safe sanitation

Key Targets

  • Achieve universal access to safe and affordable drinking water
  • Achieve access to adequate sanitation and hygiene for all
  • Improve water quality by reducing pollution and minimizing release of hazardous chemicals

Clean, accessible water for all is essential for health, education, and economic productivity.

7

Affordable and Clean Energy

Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all. Energy is central to nearly every major challenge and opportunity the world faces today.

759M
Without electricity
2.6B
Use polluting fuels

Key Targets

  • Ensure universal access to affordable, reliable and modern energy services
  • Increase substantially the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix
  • Double the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency

Transitioning to clean energy is key to combating climate change and fostering sustainable development.

8

Decent Work and Economic Growth

Promote sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all. Sustainable economic growth requires societies to create conditions for people to access quality jobs.

207M
Unemployed in 2022
1 in 5
Youth not in employment/education

Key Targets

  • Sustain per capita economic growth in accordance with national circumstances
  • Achieve higher levels of economic productivity through diversification, technological upgrading and innovation
  • Promote development-oriented policies that support productive activities and decent job creation

Decent work means productive work that delivers a fair income, security, and social protection for families.

9

Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization and foster innovation. Investments in infrastructure are crucial to achieving sustainable development.

16%
Internet access in LDCs
2.6%
Manufacturing in LDCs

Key Targets

  • Develop quality, reliable, sustainable and resilient infrastructure
  • Promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization
  • Enhance scientific research and upgrade industrial capabilities

Technological progress is key to finding lasting solutions to both economic and environmental challenges.

10

Reduced Inequality

Reduce inequality within and among countries. The international community has made significant strides towards lifting people out of poverty, but inequality persists.

71%
World population live in countries with rising inequality
26
People own as much as poorest 50%

Key Targets

  • Sustain income growth of the bottom 40% of the population at a rate higher than the national average
  • Empower and promote social, economic and political inclusion of all
  • Ensure equal opportunity and reduce inequalities of outcome

Inequality threatens long-term social cohesion and economic growth. Reducing it is essential for sustainable development.

11

Sustainable Cities and Communities

Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable. Urbanization presents opportunities for sustainable development but also challenges.

68%
Population will live in urban areas by 2050
90%
Urban waste not properly treated

Key Targets

  • Ensure access for all to adequate, safe and affordable housing and basic services
  • Provide access to safe, affordable, accessible and sustainable transport systems
  • Enhance inclusive and sustainable urbanization and capacity for participatory, integrated planning

Urban areas are hubs for ideas, commerce, culture, science, and social development, but they must be sustainable and resilient.

12

Responsible Consumption and Production

Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. Efficient management of our shared natural resources and the way we dispose of toxic waste and pollutants is essential.

1/3
Food wasted globally
1.3B tons
Municipal solid waste generated annually

Key Targets

  • Implement sustainable consumption and production patterns
  • Reduce waste generation through prevention, reduction, recycling and reuse
  • Encourage companies to adopt sustainable practices and sustainability reporting

Changing consumption patterns is essential to reduce the strain on natural resources and prevent environmental degradation.

13

Climate Action

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. Climate change is one of the greatest challenges of our time and affects everyone globally.

1.1°C
Global temperature rise since pre-industrial
20M
People displaced by climate disasters/year

Key Targets

  • Strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-related hazards
  • Integrate climate change measures into national policies, strategies, and planning
  • Improve education, awareness-raising, and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation

Urgent climate action can save lives, protect livelihoods, and ensure a sustainable planet for future generations.

14

Life Below Water

Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources. Oceans are vital for food security, climate regulation, and economic development.

33%
Fish stocks overfished
8M tons
Plastic enters oceans annually

Key Targets

  • Prevent and reduce marine pollution
  • Sustainably manage and protect marine ecosystems
  • Regulate harvesting and end overfishing

Healthy oceans are critical for biodiversity, livelihoods, and combating climate change.

15

Life on Land

Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. Combat desertification, halt biodiversity loss, and conserve forests.

1M
Species threatened with extinction
10M ha/year
Forests lost annually

Key Targets

  • Combat desertification and restore degraded land and soil
  • Ensure conservation of mountain ecosystems
  • Protect biodiversity and halt species extinction

Terrestrial ecosystems support life and human well-being, providing clean air, water, and fertile soils.

16

Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions

Promote peaceful and inclusive societies, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable institutions at all levels.

415M
People affected by conflict
59%
Global population in countries with weak institutions

Key Targets

  • Significantly reduce all forms of violence
  • End abuse, exploitation, trafficking, and violence against children
  • Promote the rule of law at national and international levels

Peaceful societies are essential for development, prosperity, and sustainable growth.

17

Partnerships for the Goals

Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development. Achieving the Sustainable Development Goals requires strong collaboration between governments, private sectors, civil society, and individuals worldwide.

$147B+
Official Development Assistance
79%
Internet Access in Developed Regions

Key Targets

  • Strengthen domestic resource mobilization with international support
  • Mobilize additional financial resources for developing countries
  • Enhance North–South, South–South and triangular cooperation
  • Promote access to environmentally sound technologies
  • Improve data monitoring and accountability systems

Sustainable development can only succeed through inclusive global partnerships built upon shared values, vision, and goals. Collaboration is the foundation for achieving all 17 SDGs.
